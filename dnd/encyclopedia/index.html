Our Heroes

Deige Stonewall

Hill Dwarf Paladin, Oath of the Ancients

Deige Stonewall, age 27 at the time our story begins, is one of our heroes. Deige joins our story in Chapter 2, intercepting and joining our heroes in the village of Nashkel after having been tasked by Candlekeep Monastery to track down the sigil sequence once the theft was discovered.

Connections:

  • Foundling left at the Order of Nyx's monastery.
  • Raised by Altair, the leader of the Order of Nyx, who was murdered.
  • Head paladin of the Order of Nyx.
  • Tasked by Candlekeep Monastery with tracking down the sigil sequence after it was stolen.
  • Defeated The Black Spider in the battle at the laboratory in Nashkel.
  • Present at the discovery of the golden scarab through a portal opened by the sigil sequence.
  • Participated in the First Annual Baldur's Gate Airship Regatta aboard The Phoenix to defeat Lord Farringwray and gain the loyalty of Captain Nathan Foulweather, who provides transport to Oman's Isle to rescue Haşmet.
  • Rescued Haşmet from his haywire laboratory on Oman's Isle in order to have Haşmet assist in researching the golden scarab.
  • Present at the heist of the Jewel of Flame from the Covelli Crime Family's casino in Athkatla and assists in delivering the jewel to Kairon in exchange for information about the sigil sequence.
  • Feigned interest in interning with TransWorld Acquisition Technologies to acquire equipment and passage to the Underdark to track down The Black Spider and determine his connection to Dagon and the sigil sequence.
  • Best known for falling into a shark tank three times in less than one minute.

Duff

Human Monk, Way of the Four Elements

Duff, age 42 at the time our story begins, is one of our heroes. Duff begins our story with us, having been hired by Gundren Rockseeker to assist in a delivery of mining supplies to the village of Nashkel. Duff, who strives toward a life of non-violence, struggles with his order's somewhat more militant outlook and bristles under rigid authority.

Connections:

  • Adherent of the Jesuvia Order, but bristles under its more militant standpoint.
  • Hired by Gundren Rockseeker to escort a load of mining equipment to the village of Nashkel.
  • Defeated The Black Spider in the battle at the laboratory in Nashkel.
  • Present at the discovery of the golden scarab through a portal opened by the sigil sequence.
  • Refuses to set foot on the grounds at Candlekeep on religious grounds.
  • Believes that the Ignatio present at the conclave following the discovery of the golden scarab may be an impostor.
  • Participated in the First Annual Baldur's Gate Airship Regatta aboard The Phoenix to defeat Lord Farringwray and gain the loyalty of Captain Nathan Foulweather, who provides transport to Oman's Isle to rescue Haşmet.
  • Brings Sekmet Nzinga into the party by bringing her along from the Jesuvia orphanage in Baldur's Gate.
  • Present at the heist of the Jewel of Flame from the Covelli Crime Family's casino in Athkatla and assists in delivering the jewel to Kairon in exchange for information about the sigil sequence.
  • Feigned interest in interning with TransWorld Acquisition Technologies to acquire equipment and passage to the Underdark to track down The Black Spider and determine his connection to Dagon and the sigil sequence.

Esmerelda

High Elf Abjuration Wizard

Esmerelda, mid-40s at the time our story begins, is one of our heroes. Esmerelda begins our story with us, having been hired by Gundren Rockseeker to assist in a delivery of mining supplies to the village of Nashkel. Esmerelda is joined by a hawk familiar named Steve Hawkington, acquired at the beginning of Chapter 3.

Connections:

  • Protégé of Zohar.
  • Hired by Gundren Rockseeker to escort a load of mining equipment to the village of Nashkel.
  • Defeated The Black Spider in the battle at the laboratory in Nashkel.
  • Present at the discovery of the golden scarab through a portal opened by the sigil sequence.
  • Has a hawk familiar named Steve Hawkington, sometimes known as "Mike Hawk".
  • Participated in the First Annual Baldur's Gate Airship Regatta aboard The Phoenix to defeat Lord Farringwray and gain the loyalty of Captain Nathan Foulweather, who provides transport to Oman's Isle to rescue Haşmet.
  • Identified by Quizbot, Your Learning Buddy within Haşmet's laboratory as (possibly?) having been a general at the Battle of Calimshan over 1000 years ago, an event Esmerelda has no knowledge of.
  • Rescued Haşmet from his haywire laboratory on Oman's Isle in order to have Haşmet assist in researching the golden scarab.
  • Present at the heist of the Jewel of Flame from the Covelli Crime Family's casino in Athkatla and assists in delivering the jewel to Kairon in exchange for information about the sigil sequence.
  • Feigned interest in interning with TransWorld Acquisition Technologies to acquire equipment and passage to the Underdark to track down The Black Spider and determine his connection to Dagon and the sigil sequence.
  • Currently in possession of the sigil sequence.

Hooligan

Human Assassin Rogue

Hooligan, age 26 at the time our story begins, is one of our heroes. Hooligan begins our story with us, having been hired by Gundren Rockseeker to assist in a delivery of mining supplies to the village of Nashkel. Hooligan, having been raised by her grandfather Michael Covelli within the Covelli crime family, struggles with the flickering shadows of a childhood scarred by violence.

Connections:

  • Born Antonio Covelli, Hooligan is transgender and is the estranged grandchild of Michael Enunciado Covelli, who raised her.
  • Hired by Gundren Rockseeker to escort a load of mining equipment to the village of Nashkel.
  • Defeated The Black Spider in the battle at the laboratory in Nashkel.
  • Present at the discovery of the golden scarab through a portal opened by the sigil sequence.
  • Participated in the First Annual Baldur's Gate Airship Regatta aboard The Phoenix to defeat Lord Farringwray and gain the loyalty of Captain Nathan Foulweather.
  • Interrogates Vinnie the Halfling, causing him to abruptly close down Vinnie's Magic Items, Holy Relics, and Pawn, and to flee from Baldur's Gate.
  • Kairon, while a member of the Covelli crime family, does not recognize Hooligan's new moniker, though he does seem to recognize her face from somewhere...
  • Present at the heist of the Jewel of Flame from the Covelli Crime Family's casino in Athkatla and assists in delivering the jewel to Kairon in exchange for information about the sigil sequence.
  • Feigned interest in interning with TransWorld Acquisition Technologies to acquire equipment and passage to the Underdark to track down The Black Spider and determine his connection to Dagon and the sigil sequence.

Noda

Half-Orc Barbarian, Path of the Totem

Noda, age 16 at the time our story begins, is formerly a member of our group of heroes. Noda begins our story with us, having been hired by Gundren Rockseeker to assist in a delivery of mining supplies to the village of Nashkel. After receiving her winnings from the airship race and with more gold than she has ever seen in her lifetime, Noda departs our story at the end of Chapter 4.

Connections:

  • Hired by Gundren Rockseeker to escort a load of mining equipment to the village of Nashkel.
  • Defeated The Black Spider in the battle at the laboratory in Nashkel.
  • Present at the discovery of the golden scarab through a portal opened by the sigil sequence.
  • Participated in the First Annual Baldur's Gate Airship Regatta aboard The Phoenix to defeat Lord Farringwray and gain the loyalty of Captain Nathan Foulweather.

Sekmet Nzinga

Half-Elf Battle Master Fighter

Sekmet, a teenager at the time our story begins, is one of our heroes. Sekmet enters our story in Chapter 6, brought in by Duff from the Jesuvia orphanage in Baldur's Gate. In spite of being young, Sekmet is already a competent warrior, though her temper can get the better of her.

Connections:

  • Brought into the group from the Jesuvia orphanage in Baldur's Gate by Duff.
  • Present at the heist of the Jewel of Flame from the Covelli Crime Family's casino in Athkatla and assists in delivering the jewel to Kairon in exchange for information about the sigil sequence.

Candlekeep Monastery

Keepers of the Silver Flame and home to the largest library on the Sword Coast, Candlekeep Monastery is an ancient and revered place of learning.

  • Employs Astra as a professor.
  • Employs Zohar as a professor.
  • Haşmet is professor emeritus at Candlekeep.
  • Esmerelda educated within.
  • Iarno educated within.
  • Formerly possessed Zohar's book and the sigil sequence within the library's restricted section. These were stolen by agents to TransWorld Acquisition Technologies under contract from the Covelli Crime Family and Dagon's order.

Astra

Astra is a human cleric and professor at Candlekeep Monastery. She is introduced into our story in Chapter 3.

Connections:

  • Professor at Candlekeep Monastery.
  • In a relationship with Zohar.
  • Although their religious precepts differ, Astra has attempted to reach out to Duff to establish a common ground.
  • Researcher of the golden scarab.

Haşmet

Haşmet is gnomish wizard and a retired professor from Candlekeep Monastery. A supposed mechanical genius, he retired to a ramshackle laboratory on Oman's isle to build a "winter wonderland" for children. Our heroes rescued him at Zohar and Astra's request when his equipment went haywire in order to assist with researching the golden scarab. Haşmet is introduced into our story in Chapter 5.

Connections:

  • Retired from his professorship at Candlekeep.
  • Former colleague of Astra and Zohar.
  • Rescued from his laboratory on Oman's isle by Esmerelda, Deige Stonewall, and Nathan Foulweather.
  • Assisting Astra and Zohar with researching the golden scarab found by Hooligan, Esmerelda, Noda, Duff, and Deige Stonewall.

Zohar

Zohar is a human wizard and professor at Candlekeep Monastery. She and Esmerelda have known each other since before the events of our story, as Esmerelda was Zohar's protégé during Esmerelda's studies at Candlekeep. Zohar appears to have a mysterious past as detailed in a book attributed to her as well as a connection to the enigmatic Dagon, but what recollection of these events she has is yet unclear. Zohar is first mentioned in our story in Chapter 2.

Connections:

  • Professor at Candlekeep Monastery.
  • In a relationship with Astra.
  • Instructor of Esmerelda and Iarno.
  • Identifies The Black Spider and Iarno as being one in the same.
  • Believes that the powers displayed by Iarno at the battle at his laboratory in Nashkel indicate that he must have some sort of powerful patron.
  • Researcher of the golden scarab.
  • Apparent author of Zohar's book and the sigil sequence.
  • Zohar's book states she was once regent of Calimshan and an empress in the era before the Great Cataclysm, over a thousand years in the past.
  • Zohar's book indicates she may have no memory of the events detailed within.
  • Has some relationship to Dagon via an unspecified bargain.

Covelli Crime Family

Lead by the Michael Covelli from the city of Athkatla, the Covelli family is a major underworld organization across much of the Sword Coast. Rumored to have operatives scattered from Waterdeep to Murann and with connections as far east as the Dalelands, much organized criminal activity in the area passes through Covelli hands at one stage or another.

  • Based out of Athkatla.
  • Lead by Michael Covelli.
  • Hooligan, now estranged, was raised within.
  • Kairon is an operative.
  • Vinnie the Halfling is an associate.
  • Uses TransWorld Acquisition Technologies as a contractor.
  • Formerly in possession of the Jewel of Flame.
  • Directed by Dagon to steal Zohar's book, extract the sigil sequence from within, and then destroy the remainder.

Kairon

Kairon is a tiefling in hiding, who poses as a bartender at a tavern in Athkatla known as The Viper Pit. In reality, Kairon is a business manager and fixer for the Covelli Crime Family.

Connections:

  • Employee of Michael Enunciado Covelli.
  • Operates the bar The Viper Pit in Athkatla.
  • Is working past his boss to serve Dagon.
  • Informed by Hooligan that Vinnie sold the sigil sequence to Gundren Rockseeker, instead of Iarno as instructed. Vinnie's life is now in severe danger.
  • Appears to find Hooligan's face familiar, but does not recognize Hooligan's name.
  • Traded our heroes information about the sigil sequence in exchange for them stealing the Jewel of Flame from Michael Covelli's casino The Tiger's Eye in order to supply the Jewel of Flame to Dagon.
  • Tells our heroes that Michael Covelli does not take Dagon particularly seriously, a mistake Kairon believes will be fatal.
  • Does not personally know Zohar, but knows she is involved with Dagon somehow.
  • Informs our heroes that Dagon is a powerful, dangerous being who may not even be mortal.
  • Hired TransWorld Acquisition Technologies to steal the sigil sequence and fence it to Iarno through Vinnie the Halfling at Dagon's request.
  • Had the first page of Zohar's book preserved counter to his instructions from Dagon to destroy the book once the sigil sequence was removed. Supplied this page to our heroes.
  • Places our heroes in contact with TransWorld Acquisition Technologies.

Michael Enunciado Covelli

Michael Covelli is a human who controls the Covelli Crime Family in the city of Athkatla.

Connections:

  • Raised Hooligan. Hooligan and Michael are now estranged.
  • Asserted by Hooligan to be violent and cruel.
  • Owner of the casino The Tiger's Eye in Athkatla.
  • Employer of Kairon.
  • Hired by Dagon to supply the sigil sequence to Iarno via Vinnie the Halfling.
  • Appears to be trying to withhold the Jewel of Flame from Dagon, possibly to extract some advantage over him.
  • Kairon finds Michael foolish for attempting to toy with Dagon and has our heroes steal the Jewel of Flame from his casino in order to supply it to Dagon, as ordered.

Vinnie the Halfling

Vinnie is a halfling who owns and operates Vinnie's Magic Items, Holy Relics, and Pawn in Baldur's Gate. After interrogation by Hooligan regarding the sigil sequence's provenance, Vinnie abruptly closed his shop and disappeared.

Connections:

  • Owns Vinnie's Magic Items, Holy Relics, and Pawn.
  • Used as a fence by the Covelli Crime Family.
  • Contracted by the Covellis to pass the sigil sequence to Iarno. Instead, Vinnie let his greed get the best of him and sold it to Gundren Rockseeker, a frequent target of Vinnie's scams.

  • Provides Hooligan the name of Kairon, his contact with the Covelli Crime Family in Athkatla.
  • After Hooligan's interrogation, Vinnie realizes his life is in danger and vanishes.

Jesuvia Order

The Jesuvia Order is a monastic order and charitable organization with outposts in major cities across the Sword Coast. Lead by the monk Ignatio, the Jesuvia Order is heterodoxical from the dominant Silver Flame and has come under some suspicion as a result. Teaches toward self-discipline and holistic education.

  • Lead by Ignatio.
  • Duff is a member, but has recently struggled with his beliefs, as the Jesuvia Order is more militant than he is comfortable adhering to.
  • Sekmet Nzinga is an orphan cared for by Jesuvia's orphanage in Baldur's Gate.

Ignatio

Ignatio is a human monk of the Jesuvia order and Duff's master.

Connections:

  • Head monk of the Jesuvia order.
  • Master of Duff.
  • At the conclave following the discovery of the golden scarab, Ignatio's behavior led Duff to wonder if his master had been replaced by an impostor.

Other Major Characters

Dagon

Dagon is a yet-mysterious figure who appears to have some connection to Zohar via an unnamed bargain.

Connections:

  • Client of Michael Covelli and Kairon.
  • Ordered the sigil sequence to be stolen from Candlekeep Monastery and provided to Iarno.
  • Demanded that Zohar's book be destroyed once the sigil sequence was separated from it.
  • The surviving first page of Zohar's book suggests she and Dagon have a past that may predate the Great Cataclysm and suggests some bargain may have been involved.

Iarno / The Black Spider

Iarno is an elvish wizard notable for his unusual greyish skin and for having given himself the moniker "The Black Spider". Our heroes first encounter Iarno in Chapter 2, investigating the underground laboratory he established in Nashkel.

Connections:

  • Former student of Zohar.
  • Considered by Zohar to not have much in the way of potential.
  • The intended recipient of the sigil sequence via Vinnie the Halfling.
  • Established a makeshift laboratory in Nashkel where he appeared to be creating monsters out of reanimated sewn-together corpses.
  • Encountered and defeated by our heroes at the battle at the laboratory in Nashkel.
  • Displayed abilities in the battle at the laboratory in Nashkel that lead Zohar to believe he may have obtained a powerful patron.
  • Rumored to have established an underground laboratory deep in the Underdark.

Nathan Foulweather

Nathan Foulweather is a middle-aged mountain dwarf and captain of the airship The Phoenix. Drunk and down on his luck, Nathan Foulweather is first encountered by our heroes in Chapter 3, where they secure passage with him to Oman's Isle to rescue Haşmet in exchange for helping Foulweather defeat Lord Farringwray in the First Annual Baldur's Gate Airship Regatta. Now an ally of our heroes, Nathan and his airship have become invaluable assets as our adventurers explore the mystery of the golden scarab they discovered.

Connections:

  • Designed Lord Farringwray's ship The Golden Legacy, but stiffed on the payment.
  • Captain of the The Phoenix, a ramshackle but sturdy airship of Nathan's own design.
  • Along with Deige Stonewall, Duff, Esmerelda, Hooligan, and Noda, defeated Lord Farringwray in the First Annual Baldur's Gate Airship Regatta, restoring Foulweather's sense of honor and winning 20,000 GP for each of our heroes.
  • Participated in the rescue of Haşmet from Oman's isle along with Esmerelda and Deige Stonewall.

Key Objects

Golden Scarab

  • Discovered by Deige Stonewall, Duff, Esmerelda, Hooligan, and Noda on the other side of a portal opened by the sigil sequence.
  • Perhaps 100 feet in length and appearing to be made of gold, the scarab's purpose and significance remains unknown.
  • Appears to have greatly perturbed Zohar.
  • Zohar and Haşmet believe the golden scarab may predate The Great Cataclysm.
  • Under active investigation by Astra, Zohar, and Haşmet at a makeshift research center in Baldur's Gate.

Jewel of Flame

  • Stolen from the possession of Michael Covelli by Deige Stonewall, Duff, Esmerelda, Hooligan, and Sekmet Nzinga as part of a bargain with Kairon.
  • Formerly stored in the most secure vault in Michael Covelli's casino, within a piercing light that withers anything it touches.
  • Formerly kept in a small box not black in color, but with the appearance of nothing. Touching the box with the jewel inside gave Duff a feeling of immense dread. The box itself does not have this power once the jewel is removed.
  • Has the appearance of swirling orange and red flame licking across the surface.
  • Provided to Kairon, who intimates that Dagon is the intended recipient.
  • Appears to have been kept from Dagon by Michael Covelli. Kairon suggests that Michael Covelli may be attempting to toy with Dagon or extract some advantage from him through this, a choice Kairon believes dangerous and foolish.

Sigil Sequence

  • A sequence of eleven sigils, organized as a portal key.
  • Stolen from Candlekeep Monastery by agents of TransWorld Acquisition Technologies on behalf of the Covelli Crime Family, apparently at Dagon's request.
  • Provided to Vinnie the Halfling, who was intended to fence it to Iarno, but instead sold it to Gundren Rockseeker.
  • Sold to Gundren as a "map to the lost mine of Nashkel", prompting Gundren to hire Duff, Esmerelda, Noda, and Hooligan to assist in delivering mining supplies to Nashkel at the beginning of our story.
  • Deige Stonewall engaged to track down sigil sequence on behalf of Candlekeep once the theft is discovered.
  • Deige believes the map may have celestial connections.
  • Esmerelda recognizes that although all known sigil sequences have the same ninth, tenth, and eleventh runes, this sequence has a unique ninth rune.
  • Opens a portal to a hypostyle hall containing the golden scarab.
  • The paper the sigil sequence is printed on appears to be very ancient.

Zohar's Book

  • Claims to have been written by Zohar.
  • Appears to be very ancient.
  • Stolen from Candlekeep Monastery by agents of TransWorld Acquisition Technologies under contract from the Covelli Crime Family, apparently at Dagon's order.
  • Ordered destroyed by Dagon.
  • First page preserved by Kairon, which reads:

    • I, Zohar, once called the Regent of Calimshan and Empress of the known lands east of the Trackless Sea, do commit now to these pages the last testament of the world in the days before the Great Cataclysm that befell it, the last memories of the bargain I made, and how in my weakness I failed the world and caused all its wonders to be destroyed. Even now as I write these words, my recollection of these last days fades, torn from my mind by the lengths to which I went to cowardly avoid the consequences of my actions. To whomever finds and reads these words, I beg of you to trust not he who calls himself Dagon, for behind his fair words is the utmost of malevolence.

  • The sigil sequence was ripped from its pages.

Major Events

The Battle of Calimshan

  • Occurred in the year 392 DR. It is currently 1479 DR.
  • Given the name, apparently took place in Calimshan.
  • According to QuizBot, Your Learning Buddy (a machine in Haşmet's lab), the generals present included:
    • King Umran Ironhelm of the Greypeak Mountains
    • Arcturas, Dragon-Marked of House Turok
    • Possibly: Esmerelda, Protégé of Zohar the Magnificent

The Great Cataclysm

  • An apocalyptic event that occurred over a millennium ago
  • Details of this event are as yet unclear.
  • The subject of Zohar's life's research.

Other Locations and Organizations

Athkatla

A beautiful Italianate city on the southern edge of the Sword Coast.

  • Home to the Covelli Crime Family's center of operations, The Tiger's Eye casino.
  • Location of The Viper Pit, Kairon's bar.
  • Location of a TransWorld Acquisition Technologies outpost.
  • Home to The Yawning Portal, a tavern and one of the few known entrances to the Underdark.

Baldur's Gate

This great walled city has stood for countless generations, its grey stone walls and monumental architecture believed to be impregnable.

  • Beginning point in our story for Duff, Esmerelda, Hooligan, and Noda.
  • Home to the now-shuttered Vinnie's Magic Items, Holy Relics, and Pawn.
  • Location of a hastily-assembled research group investigating the golden scarab, including Astra, Zohar, and Haşmet.

Calimshan

Once a powerful city state in the desert far to the south, Calimshan has been a ruin as long as any can remember.

  • Listed as the site of the Battle of Calimshan, which QuizBot, Your Learning Buddy (a machine in Haşmet's lab) thinks may have included Esmerelda as a general.

TransWorld Acquisition Technologies, a division of ConHugeCo Industries

This organization is used as a contractor for the Covelli family, handling "acquisition" of items and offering the Covellis plausible deniability.

  • Contracted by Kairon to steal Zohar's book and the sigil sequence within from Candlekeep Monastery on the orders of Dagon.
  • Deige Stonewall, Duff, Esmerelda, and Hooligan posed as prospective interns to gain access to equipment and maps to the Underdark.

The Underdark

Believed by most to be mythical, the Underdark is a network of caverns far below the Sword Coast. To describe it as vast is an understatement - though almost entirely unmapped, the Underdark is believed contain tunnels that stretch as far north as Neverwinter and perhaps as far east as Turmish and the Dalelands. To those few overlanders who have seen it, the Underdark is home to innumerable nameless and monstrous creatures, treacherous cities filled with slave traders, and above all else, ceaseless, maddening darkness and cold.

  • Rumors indicate Iarno may have established a base of operations somewhere in the Underdark.
  • TransWorld Acquisition Technologies has an operative who makes occasional expeditions to the city of Erelhei-Cinlu in the Underdark.
  • Accessible via The Yawning Portal, a tavern in Athkatla.
